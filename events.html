EVENTS

Fall Quarter

Zero Week Picnic

Zero Week Picnic is the first event of the year! You'll be able to meet new people, enjoy some BOMB FOOD, and win some FREE T-SHIRTS. You'll also be able to meet many of our NSU members and they will share all of their crazy memories with you!

Bonfire

Food, Friends, (Bon)Fire. Pretty much all you need on a Saturday evening. Come out to NSU's annual Bonfire to cook some s’mores, get sand everywhere, and have a ridiculous amount of fun on the beach!

Premiere

NSU's annual Fu's clubbing event is the perfect opportunity to meet new people and let loose at the end of the week. The theme is always Highlighter, so wear a white shirt, bring a highlighter, and feel free to write whatever you want on the cool people you meet throughout the night!

Co-ed Tournament

NSU’s annual basketball tournament. basketball. ball ball something ball baskeball.

Meltdown

The biggest event of fall quarter! Unwind at INC’s Meltdown, where NSUs from all of Southern California gather to dance away their worries.

Ski Trip

(Almost) Every year NSU makes the annual pilgrimage out to some snowy mountain. Race fellow NSU members down mountains and then cozy up with them at night! There’s nothing better than hot chocolate and snow.

Spring Quarter

Spring Retreat

Come enjoy a weekend of fun and leadership-building with your favorite NSUers! Find out if you want to apply for NSU Staff/Board for the next school year!

YIM

The Yuji Ichioka Memorial Tournament is held every year in honor of Yuki Ichioka, a Japanese American Civil Rights Activist. Come play ball with NSU teams from all over California.

Fiesta Matsuri

Fiesta Matsuri is a community service event held annually at Little Tokyo. The event combines the Children's Day celebration of two communities - the Japanese Kodomo no Hi and the Latino Dia de los Niños. A multi-ethnic celebration for children and their families including arts & crafts workshops, games, food and performances on the plaza.

Manzanar

Interested in Japanese internment history? Then come out to NSU’s annual Manzanar Pilgrimage! Learn about the history of Japanese incarceration during World War II at the Manzanar interpretive center and memorial service. We’ll have a thoughtful discussion during our Manzanar at Dusk Program afterwards, and spend the remainder the night bonding together and camping out under the stars.
