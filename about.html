Why Does NSU Exist?

Origin

Founded in 1981, NSU has become established in the Japanese American community—not only as a social club for connecting the collegiate Nikkei student population, but as a politically powerful and community-oriented unit.

Our Vision

NSU hopes to create a fun social community for Japanese Americans and those interested in Japanese culture.

Our Mission

The NSU mission statement vows to educate the public on Japanese American culture and promote political awareness.

Our History

Shortly after its conception, NSU became heavily involved in the Redress Commission hearings in Los Angeles regarding the Japanese American incarceration during World War II. In February of 1986, NSU hosted its first “Week of Remembrance,” an on-campus event with the purpose of educating the general student body on the history of Japanese Americans and, most importantly, commemorating the signing of Executive Order 9066 in 1942—the order responsible for incarcertion. Over time, the event transformed into what we now recognize as Cultural Night (CN), a student-operated performance that showcases the talent and relevant issues of the Japanese American community.
